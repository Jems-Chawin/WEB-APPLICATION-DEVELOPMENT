biceps curl


The bicep curl is a classic isolation exercise targeting the biceps muscles in the front of your upper arms. To perform a bicep curl, you typically use dumbbells or a barbell. Start with your arms fully extended at your sides, holding the weights with an underhand grip. Keeping your upper arms stationary, curl the weights upward by contracting your biceps. Slowly lower the weights back down to the starting position.

tricep extension


This exercise focuses on the triceps, the muscles at the back of your upper arms. You can use a dumbbell, barbell, or specialized cable machine for this exercise. Start with the weight held above your head, then lower it behind your head by bending your elbows. Extend your elbows to raise the weight back up to the starting position, focusing on the contraction of your triceps.

bench press


The bench press is a compound exercise that primarily targets the chest muscles (pectorals), but it also engages the shoulders and triceps. Lie flat on a bench, gripping a barbell with hands slightly wider than shoulder-width apart. Lower the barbell to your chest by bending your elbows, then push it back up to the starting position by extending your arms.

shoulders press


The shoulder press targets the deltoid muscles in your shoulders and also engages the triceps. You can perform it with dumbbells or a barbell. Start with the weights at shoulder height, then press them overhead by extending your arms. Lower the weights back to shoulder height to complete one repetition.

pull ups


Pull-ups are a bodyweight exercise that primarily work the back muscles, particularly the latissimus dorsi ("lats"). Hang from a pull-up bar with your palms facing away from you, hands slightly wider than shoulder-width apart. Pull yourself up by engaging your back muscles and bending your elbows, then lower yourself back down with control.

squat


Squats are a compound lower body exercise that target the quadriceps, hamstrings, and glutes. Stand with your feet shoulder-width apart, and lower your body by bending your knees and hips as if sitting back into an imaginary chair. Keep your back straight and chest up. Go as low as your flexibility allows, then push through your heels to return to the starting position.

leg raise


Leg raises are a bodyweight exercise primarily targeting the lower abdominal muscles. Lie flat on your back with your arms at your sides or under your hips for support. Keeping your legs straight, lift them off the ground by contracting your lower abs. Lower your legs back down without letting them touch the floor to complete one repetition.
